# Field Operations Specialist

## Role Title

Field Operations Specialist — front-line operator who deploys and manages anvil installations in the field using AnvilOS.

## Role Context

Reports to the Operations Manager at medium-to-large predator solution providers (50-200 employees). Typically works in remote, harsh environments — desert canyons, rocky outcrops, and arid plateaus. Teams of 2-4 operators cover a geographic territory, rotating between client sites. Most operators have 3-10 years of field experience, often starting in manual deployment before transitioning to tech-assisted operations.

## Core Responsibilities

- Deploy anvils at designated drop points using AnvilOS targeting guidance
- Configure targeting parameters for terrain, wind, and target species
- Perform pre-deployment safety checks and post-deployment assessments
- Maintain field equipment (catapults, launchers, GPS units)
- Log deployment outcomes and capture rate data in AnvilOS
- Coordinate with the Operations Manager on daily schedules and priority targets

## Goals

- High capture rates per deployment (target: 85%+ accuracy)
- Zero safety incidents per quarter
- Efficient equipment utilisation — minimise setup/teardown time
- Complete all scheduled deployments within shift windows
- Accurate data capture for performance reporting

## Pain Points

- **AnvilOS targeting accuracy**: v1.0 targeting algorithm struggles with canyon wind patterns, requiring manual override ~30% of the time
- **Setup time**: Equipment calibration takes 25-40 minutes per site; operators feel this should be under 15 minutes
- **Connectivity**: Many deployment zones have no cellular coverage; AnvilOS requires workarounds for offline operation
- **Data entry burden**: Post-deployment logging is manual and repetitive — operators often batch it at end of day, reducing data quality
- **Weather adaptation**: No real-time weather integration means operators rely on personal judgement for wind adjustments

## Tools & Systems

- AnvilOS mobile app (primary — 4-6 hours daily)
- GPS tracking units (Garmin fleet devices)
- Catapult control systems (CatapultPro hardware)
- Two-way field radios
- Paper logbooks (backup when offline)
- Personal weather stations (some operators bring their own)

## Success Metrics

- Capture rate per deployment (target: 85%)
- Deployments completed per shift
- Equipment downtime hours
- Safety incident count (target: zero)
- Data completeness score (% of deployments fully logged same-day)

## Product Interaction

Daily user, 4-6 hours per day in AnvilOS. Heavy user of targeting and deployment features. Rarely uses analytics or reporting features (that is the Operations Manager's domain). Accesses AnvilOS exclusively on ruggedised tablets in the field. Most operators use a subset of features and develop personal workflows around AnvilOS limitations.

## Adoption Barriers

- **Trust in technology**: Experienced operators trust field instinct over algorithmic targeting, especially after early v1.0 misses
- **Connectivity constraints**: Offline mode is limited, making operators reluctant to depend on the app in remote areas
- **Learning curve**: AI-assisted targeting requires understanding confidence scores and parameter tuning — not intuitive for operators trained on manual methods
- **Device durability**: Tablets fail in extreme heat and dust; operators revert to manual methods when hardware fails

## Key Needs from Product

**Must-haves:**
- Reliable offline mode with full targeting capability
- Faster targeting calculations (sub-5-second response)
- Simplified post-deployment logging (auto-capture where possible)
- Better terrain data integration for canyon environments

**Nice-to-haves:**
- Real-time weather data integration
- Voice-activated logging for hands-free operation
- Predictive equipment maintenance alerts
- Cross-operator deployment coordination on shared map
