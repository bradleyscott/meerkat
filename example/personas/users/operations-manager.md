# Operations Manager

## Role Title

Operations Manager — oversees a team of field operators, manages client relationships, and uses AnvilOS for planning, scheduling, and performance analytics.

## Role Context

Reports to the Regional Director at medium-to-large predator solution providers. Manages 8-15 field operators across a geographic territory. Splits time between office (planning, reporting, client meetings) and occasional field visits. Typically promoted from senior field operator roles, with 8-15 years of industry experience. Responsible for both operational performance and client satisfaction.

## Core Responsibilities

- Plan and schedule daily deployment assignments across the team
- Monitor real-time team performance and deployment outcomes
- Generate weekly and monthly performance reports for clients and leadership
- Manage client relationships — regular check-ins, SLA reviews, issue resolution
- Conduct equipment procurement and maintenance scheduling
- Hire, train, and develop field operators
- Ensure compliance with safety regulations and environmental permits

## Goals

- Meet or exceed client SLAs (capture rate, response time, coverage area)
- Optimise team utilisation — minimise idle time and travel between sites
- Reduce equipment downtime through proactive maintenance
- Improve team retention (industry average turnover is high)
- Deliver accurate, timely reporting to clients and leadership
- Control operational costs within budget

## Pain Points

- **Lack of real-time visibility**: Cannot see live deployment status; relies on radio check-ins and end-of-day logs
- **Manual reporting**: Weekly client reports require exporting data from AnvilOS, reformatting in spreadsheets, and adding commentary — takes 3-4 hours per client
- **Scheduling inefficiency**: No route optimisation; scheduling is done manually on whiteboards and shared calendars
- **Data quality issues**: Operators batch-enter deployment logs, leading to gaps and inaccuracies that surface during client reviews
- **Equipment tracking**: No centralised view of equipment condition, location, or maintenance schedules
- **Forecast difficulty**: Cannot predict team capacity or equipment needs more than a week ahead

## Tools & Systems

- AnvilOS desktop dashboard (primary — 3-4 hours daily for planning and reporting)
- AnvilOS mobile app (occasional — field visits only)
- Microsoft Excel (reporting, budgeting, scheduling)
- Google Calendar (team scheduling)
- Salesforce (client relationship management)
- Email and phone (client communication)
- Whiteboard (deployment scheduling — physical, in the office)

## Success Metrics

- Client SLA compliance rate (target: 95%+)
- Team utilisation rate (target: 80%+ of shift hours productive)
- Average capture rate across team
- Equipment uptime percentage
- Client satisfaction scores (quarterly surveys)
- Reporting accuracy and timeliness
- Team retention rate

## Product Interaction

Daily user, 3-4 hours on the AnvilOS desktop dashboard. Primary consumer of analytics, team performance, and historical data features. Uses planning tools for scheduling but finds them limited. Rarely uses targeting or deployment features directly. Generates exports for client reporting. Accesses AnvilOS from office desktop and occasionally from laptop during client meetings.

## Adoption Barriers

- **Reporting gaps**: AnvilOS analytics do not match client reporting requirements, forcing manual workarounds in Excel
- **Integration absence**: No connection between AnvilOS and Salesforce (CRM) or calendar tools means double-entry of scheduling data
- **Dashboard limitations**: Cannot customise dashboards for different clients or KPIs; one-size-fits-all view
- **Trust in data**: Knows that operator-entered data has quality issues, making them cautious about sharing AnvilOS reports directly with clients

## Key Needs from Product

**Must-haves:**
- Real-time team status dashboard (who is deployed where, current status)
- Automated client-ready reporting with configurable templates
- Route and schedule optimisation tools
- Data quality indicators (flag missing or anomalous entries)

**Nice-to-haves:**
- CRM integration (Salesforce sync for client data)
- Predictive capacity planning (forecast team/equipment needs)
- Equipment lifecycle management module
- Mobile-friendly manager dashboard for field visits
