# Procurement Director

## Role Title

Procurement Director — senior leader responsible for technology and equipment purchasing decisions at enterprise predator solution providers.

## Role Context

Reports to the CFO or COO at large predator solution providers (200+ employees, $50M+ revenue). Budget authority for technology and equipment purchases over $50K. Manages a small procurement team (2-4 people) that handles vendor evaluation, contract negotiation, and ongoing vendor management. Typically has 15+ years of experience in operations or supply chain management, with 5+ years in a senior procurement role. Responsible for total cost of ownership, not just purchase price.

## Business Objectives

- Reduce total cost of operations through technology-enabled efficiency gains
- Ensure operational continuity — no technology failures that disrupt client service
- Meet compliance requirements (safety regulations, environmental permits, data privacy)
- Consolidate vendor relationships — fewer vendors, deeper partnerships, better terms
- Demonstrate measurable ROI on technology investments to the board
- Support the company's growth strategy (new territories, new service lines) with scalable solutions

## Buying Triggers

- **Contract renewals**: Existing vendor contracts approaching renewal are an opportunity to evaluate alternatives
- **Safety incidents**: Any equipment-related safety incident triggers immediate review of technology and processes
- **Competitive pressure**: When competitors demonstrate superior technology to shared clients
- **Regulatory changes**: New safety or environmental regulations requiring technology upgrades
- **Growth milestones**: Entering new territories or winning large contracts that exceed current system capacity
- **Operator complaints**: Sustained feedback from field teams about tool limitations affecting productivity

## Decision Criteria

1. **Total cost of ownership** — not just licence fees but implementation, training, maintenance, and productivity impact
2. **Reliability and uptime** — field operations cannot tolerate system downtime; SLAs must be contractually guaranteed
3. **Safety and compliance** — must meet all relevant regulations without manual workarounds
4. **Integration capability** — must work with existing CRM (Salesforce), ERP, and fleet management systems
5. **Scalability** — must support growth from current operations to 2-3x scale without re-platforming
6. **Vendor stability** — preference for established vendors with strong financials; cautious about startups
7. **Support quality** — dedicated account management and responsive technical support, not just a ticket queue

## Buying Process Role

**Primary decision-maker** for technology purchases under $200K. For larger investments, acts as the **champion and recommender** to the executive committee (CEO, CFO, COO). Has veto power on any technology procurement regardless of who initiates the request. Relies heavily on input from the Operations Director (who represents end-user needs) and the IT Director (who assesses technical fit).

## Evaluation Methods

- **Structured RFP process** for purchases over $100K
- **Pilot programmes**: Requires a 60-90 day pilot in one territory before committing to enterprise rollout
- **Reference calls**: Speaks with 3-5 existing customers in similar industries and at similar scale
- **Analyst reports**: Reads industry analyst assessments (Desert Tech Quarterly, Predator Solutions Review)
- **Total cost modelling**: Builds detailed 3-year TCO models comparing alternatives
- **Site visits**: Visits vendor offices and at least one customer deployment before signing

## Stakeholders Involved

| Stakeholder | Role in Decision | Key Concerns |
|---|---|---|
| **Operations Director** | Influencer — represents field team needs | Usability, feature coverage, operator adoption |
| **IT Director** | Influencer — assesses technical fit | Integration, security, infrastructure requirements |
| **CFO** | Approver for purchases over $200K | ROI, budget impact, contract terms |
| **Legal Counsel** | Gatekeeper — reviews contracts | Liability, data privacy, IP ownership |
| **Field Operations Manager** | Influencer — provides ground-level feedback | Day-to-day usability, training burden |

## Common Objections

- "We have already invested heavily in our current platform — what is the switching cost and risk?"
- "What happens if AnvilOS goes down in the field? Our operators need guaranteed uptime."
- "Your company is smaller than our current vendor. How do I know you will still be around in 5 years?"
- "Can you match our existing contract terms? We have favourable pricing locked in."
- "Our IT team has concerns about cloud-based targeting data. Where is our data stored and who has access?"
- "We need this to integrate with Salesforce and our ERP. Out-of-the-box, not custom development."
