# Role Context

This directory is for files that describe your specific role, responsibilities, and context. Skills use this to tailor their recommendations to your situation.

## What to Include

- **Your role and scope** — What you're responsible for, your team structure, reporting lines
- **Your influence boundaries** — What you can decide independently vs. what needs approval
- **Current priorities** — What you're focused on this quarter
- **Stakeholder context** — Key relationships, decision-makers, political dynamics
- **Personal constraints** — Budget, team size, timeline pressures

## Why This Matters

When skills like the opportunity interviewer or assumption identifier read your role context, they can:

- Ask sharper questions (e.g., "given your team of 3, is this scope realistic?")
- Recommend appropriate validation techniques (e.g., guerrilla testing vs. formal user research)
- Calibrate ambition to your influence level
- Account for organisational constraints in their recommendations

## Example

```markdown
---
title: Role Context
---

# Role Context

## Current Role
Senior Product Manager, AnvilOS Platform — responsible for the core targeting and
delivery management capabilities.

## Team
- 2 product designers
- 1 data analyst
- Shared engineering team (12 engineers across 3 squads)

## Scope
Own the AnvilOS roadmap, backlog prioritisation, and stakeholder alignment. Report
to VP Product. Dotted line to CTO on technical architecture decisions.

## Current Focus
- AnvilOS 2.0 launch (AI-powered targeting)
- Enterprise client migration programme
- Reliability improvement initiative (cross-functional)

## Influence Boundaries
- **Own decisions**: Backlog prioritisation, sprint goals, feature scoping, UX direction
- **Shared decisions**: Roadmap commitments (with VP Product), technical architecture (with CTO), pricing changes (with Commercial)
- **Need approval**: Headcount changes, budget above $20k, external partnerships, public commitments to enterprise clients

## Stakeholder Context
- **VP Product (Sarah)** — Direct manager, supportive but stretched thin across 4 PMs. Prefers async updates, flags risks early.
- **CTO (James)** — Strong opinions on architecture, wants to be consulted on anything touching the data pipeline. Ally on AI investment.
- **Head of Sales (Priya)** — Key partner for enterprise feedback. Tends to escalate client requests as urgent — need to filter signal from noise.
- **Engineering Lead (Tom)** — Runs the 3 squads day-to-day. Pragmatic, values clear requirements. Push back on scope creep comes through him.

## Personal Constraints
- **Budget**: $150k discretionary for H1; $40k already committed to analytics tooling
- **Team capacity**: Designers are at capacity until March; data analyst splits time 60/40 with Growth team
- **Timeline pressure**: AnvilOS 2.0 committed to board for Q3 launch — limited flexibility on date
- **Technical debt**: Legacy targeting engine must be maintained in parallel until migration completes (estimated 6 months)
```
