# Revenue Model

## Revenue Streams

### Recurring Revenue (~53% of total, growing at 16% YoY)

| Stream | Description | Pricing Model |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| AnvilOS Subscriptions | Cloud platform for targeting, deployment management, and analytics | $2K–$15K/month based on deployment volume |
| Anvil-as-a-Service | Subscription-based anvil delivery — unlimited drops within plan tier | $5K–$25K/month (3 tiers: Bronze, Silver, Platinum) |
| Gadget Marketplace Commission | 15% take rate on third-party gadget sales through the platform | Per-transaction |
| Support & Maintenance | Annual contracts for hardware servicing and software support | 18-22% of initial purchase price annually |
| Managed Services | Full-service catching operations — Acme handles everything | Custom pricing, typically $50K–$200K/year |

### Non-Recurring Revenue (~47% of total, declining as % of mix)

| Stream | Description | Pricing Model |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Hardware Sales | Anvils, catapults, explosives, giant rubber bands | Per-unit pricing; anvils from $500–$5,000 |
| Implementation Services | Custom catapult installations, terrain assessment | Project-based; typically $10K–$50K |
| Professional Services | Trajectory consulting, deployment strategy | Day-rate ($2,500/day) or fixed-price engagements |
| Training | "Catching Academy" certification programmes | $5K per attendee (2-week programme) |

## Business Model Evolution

- Shifting from hardware-first to platform-first revenue model
- AnvilOS subscriptions are the primary growth driver
- Hardware becoming a "land" motion — clients buy anvils, then upgrade to AnvilOS
- Gadget Marketplace creating a flywheel effect (more gadgets → more clients → more gadgets)
- Managed Services providing high-touch option for enterprise clients
