## Head-to-Head: Acme vs. Coyote Tech

### Coyote Tech at a Glance

- **Valuation**: $400M (Series C, 2025)
- **Clients**: 45
- **Revenue**: ~$18M ARR
- **Architecture**: AI drones, cloud platform (GCP)
- **Key Limitation**: 12% success rate in desert; 45-minute battery life

### Where Acme Wins Against Coyote Tech

| Dimension | Acme Advantage | Coyote Tech Limitation |
|-----------|----------------|------------------------|
| **Field Reliability** | 18% success rate with AnvilOS; proven across all terrain types | 12% in desert conditions; GPS and thermal issues in canyons |
| **Product Breadth** | Full suite: anvils, catapults, explosives, gadgets, software | Drones only — no hardware alternatives when drones fail |
| **Cost** | AnvilOS from $2K/month; per-drop from $120 | Drone fleet from $15K/unit + $3K–$8K/month platform |
| **Battery/Endurance** | No power limitations — anvils don't need charging | 45-minute flight time per drone; extended pursuits impossible |
| **Domain Expertise** | 75 years of predator-prey domain knowledge | Founded 2021; limited field experience |
| **Track Record** | 200+ enterprise clients over decades | 45 clients, 4 years of operation |

### Talking Points for Coyote Tech Competitive Situations

1. **"12% vs. 18%."** In field conditions — the conditions that matter — our AnvilOS-guided solutions outperform their autonomous drones. Autonomy is a means, not an end. Results are what count.

2. **"What happens when the drone fails?"** Coyote Tech has one product. When it doesn't work — and in desert canyons, it usually doesn't — there's no Plan B. Acme offers anvils, catapults, explosives, gadgets, and software. We always have a backup.

3. **"45 minutes."** That's their battery life. Extended pursuits requiring patience and persistence? Not possible with drones. Our hardware doesn't run out of batteries.

4. **"They hired our sales guy, not our engineers."** Dash Runner knows our client list, but he didn't build our products. The technology advantage stays with us.

## Head-to-Head: Acme vs. Roadrunner Catch Zones

### Roadrunner Systems at a Glance

- **Valuation**: ~$1.2B (PE-backed)
- **Revenue**: $300M (Catch Zones: $75M and growing)
- **Model**: Controlled environments with 95% guaranteed catch rate
- **Locations**: 15 (expanding to 25 by end of 2026)
- **Key Concern**: Wile E. Coyote is now a Catch Zone member

### Where Acme Wins Against Roadrunner Catch Zones

| Dimension | Acme Advantage | Catch Zone Limitation |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Cost** | Per-drop from $120; AnvilOS from $2K/month | $5K–$25K per catch; $200K–$1M annual memberships |
| **Geographic Freedom** | Hunt anywhere in the world | Limited to 15 physical locations |
| **Availability** | Deploy anytime, no booking required | Booking-based; limited slots per day |
| **Autonomy & Skill** | Predators hone their craft and instincts | Controlled environment — no real skill development |
| **Scalability** | Add clients without physical infrastructure | Each new location requires massive capital investment |

### Talking Points for Roadrunner Catch Zone Competitive Situations

1. **"$5,000 minimum for a single catch."** That's 40x the cost of an AnvilOS-guided anvil drop. Catch Zones are luxury — we're practical.

2. **"15 locations worldwide."** If you're not near a Catch Zone, you're out of luck. Acme works anywhere you need to hunt — desert, mountain, forest, urban.

3. **"Do you want to hunt, or do you want it handed to you?"** Catch Zones are a controlled experience, not real hunting. Predators who value skill, independence, and the thrill of the chase choose Acme.

4. **"They're a prey company selling to predators."** Roadrunner Systems built their business helping prey escape. Now they're selling guaranteed catches? Ask yourself whose interests they really optimise for.
