## Objective 1: Launch AnvilOS 2.0 Platform

**Goal:** Successfully launch AnvilOS 2.0 with AI-powered targeting to accelerate the subscription revenue shift

**Owner:** Pat Hammer

**Why it's important:**

- AnvilOS is the centrepiece of our digital transformation strategy.
- Recurring revenue growth depends on migrating enterprise clients from hardware purchases to platform subscriptions.

**Key Results:**

1. AnvilOS 2.0 launches with AI-powered predictive targeting by end of Q1.
2. 15 enterprise clients migrated from legacy hardware-only contracts to AnvilOS subscriptions.
3. Platform uptime maintained at 99.9% during the migration period.
4. AI targeting achieves 22% success rate in field trials (up from 18% baseline).

## Objective 2: Close the Reliability Gap

**Goal:** Reduce product failure rates to rebuild customer trust and slow defection to competitors

**Owner:** Sam Catapult

**Why it's important:**

- Product failures are the #1 driver of customer churn and the primary reason clients explore alternatives like Coyote Tech drones and Roadrunner Catch Zones.
- Wile E. Coyote's Catch Zone membership is a wake-up call.

**Key Results:**

1. Reduce explosive product failure rate from 34% to below 20%.
2. Implement post-deployment failure analysis automation — root cause identified within 24 hours for all incidents.
3. Launch the Customer Success programme with our top 10 clients.
4. Achieve ISO 9001 certification for the Toontown manufacturing facility.

## Objective 3: Enter the Urban Pest Control Market

**Goal:** Establish a credible presence in urban pest control to diversify beyond desert predator operations

**Owner:** Pat Hammer

**Why it's important:**

- Urban pest control is a $2B TAM where Coyote Tech already has traction with drones.
- Our current product suite is poorly suited to urban environments — we need adapted solutions.

**Key Results:**

1. Ship a lightweight, urban-safe version of AnvilOS targeting (no explosives, noise-dampened anvils).
2. Sign 3 urban pest control pilot customers.
3. Achieve a 15% success rate in urban field trials.
