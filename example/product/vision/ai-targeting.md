# Vision: AI-Powered Predictive Targeting

## The Opportunity

Acme's current targeting relies on manual aim and gravity — a method largely unchanged since 1949. Success rates hover around 18%, compared to Coyote Tech's 12% with autonomous drones. Both numbers are poor. The predator who can reliably predict prey movement and optimise delivery timing will dominate the next decade of the catching industry.

AI-powered predictive targeting is Acme's bet on doing exactly this — and doing it with 75 years of proprietary deployment data that no competitor can replicate.

## The Hypothesis

**We believe that** applying machine learning to Acme's historical deployment data (75 years, 2M+ recorded drops) **will allow us to** predict prey movement patterns and optimise drop timing with significantly higher accuracy **which will result in** a step-change in success rates that justifies the shift from hardware sales to platform subscription pricing.

## What Success Looks Like

| Timeframe | Metric | Target |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| H1 FY26 | Field trial accuracy | 22% (from 18% baseline) |
| H2 FY26 | Production accuracy with weather/terrain data | 30% |
| FY27 | Multi-species accuracy | 35% across top 5 prey species |
| FY28 | Industry-leading accuracy | 40%+ — meaningfully ahead of any competitor |

At 30%+ accuracy, AnvilOS becomes the clear market leader in targeting effectiveness and the subscription pricing model becomes defensible.

## Strategic Fit

AI targeting sits at the intersection of two company strategic pillars:

1. **Digital Transformation** — It's the core value proposition driving AnvilOS subscriptions. Without meaningful accuracy improvement, the platform is just a management dashboard.
2. **Reliability Revolution** — Better targeting means fewer wasted drops, fewer explosions in the wrong place, and fewer product failures. AI targeting is reliability through intelligence rather than just quality control.

## Key Unknowns

| Unknown | Why it matters | How we'll learn |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| Is historical data sufficient for training? | 75 years of data, but quality varies. Pre-2010 records are paper-based and partially digitised. | Data quality audit in Q1 — assess what % of historical records are ML-ready |
| Can the model generalise across terrain? | Desert training data may not transfer to urban or forest environments | Cross-terrain validation during H2 field trials |
| Will customers trust AI-guided drops? | Predators may resist ceding targeting control to an algorithm | Pilot programme with 5 early-adopter clients; measure adoption friction |
| Does accuracy translate to willingness to pay? | 22% vs 18% may not feel meaningful enough to justify subscription pricing | Conjoint analysis during enterprise migration conversations |
| Can we maintain accuracy at scale? | Model may degrade as more clients create prediction contention in overlapping territories | Load testing and territory-based model partitioning research |

## Competitive Context

- **Coyote Tech** is approaching the same problem with autonomous drones + real-time AI. Their accuracy is currently lower (12%), but their approach scales differently — they don't need historical data because drones learn in real-time.
- **Roadrunner Catch Zones** bypass targeting entirely with controlled environments (95% success). They prove the market values reliability, but at a price point most customers can't sustain.
- **Our advantage** is the proprietary data moat. No competitor has 75 years of deployment records. If the data is as valuable as we believe, our accuracy ceiling is higher.

## Risks

- **Data moat may be weaker than assumed.** If real-time sensor data (Coyote Tech's approach) outperforms historical pattern data, our advantage evaporates.
- **Model accuracy plateau.** ML improvements are non-linear — going from 18% to 25% may be straightforward, but 25% to 35% could require fundamentally different approaches.
- **Ethical considerations.** AI-guided targeting of living creatures will attract scrutiny. Need a clear responsible-use framework before scaling.
