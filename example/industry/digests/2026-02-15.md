### OSHA Proposes Mandatory Safety Certification for Explosive Catching Devices

| | |
|---|---|
| **Topic** | OSHA and Wildlife Regulatory Changes |
| **Date** | 2026-02-10 |
| **Source** | [OSHA Federal Register Notice](https://osha.example.gov/catching/2026-certification) |

OSHA published a proposed rule requiring all explosive catching devices to carry a new "Safe Catch" certification by January 2027. The rule covers TNT, dynamite bundles, and controlled detonation services — all core Acme product lines. The comment period closes March 31, 2026. The certification requires third-party testing at an OSHA-approved lab, with an estimated compliance cost of $200K–$500K per product line.

**So what:**

- Acme's Explosive Solutions product line (TNT, dynamite bundles, controlled detonation services) all fall within scope — engineering and compliance teams need to assess readiness immediately
- The January 2027 deadline aligns with our Reliability Revolution programme — this regulation could accelerate certification efforts already planned under the ISO 9001 initiative
- Smaller competitors and startups without compliance infrastructure will face a significant barrier to entry — this could strengthen Acme's market position if we achieve certification early

**Recommended response:**

- Brief the compliance team this week; submit a public comment during the comment period (closes March 31)
- Assess whether current Reliability Revolution timelines need to accelerate to meet the January 2027 deadline

| **Topic** | Catching Industry Market Dynamics |
| **Date** | 2026-02-08 |
| **Source** | [Guidehouse Press Release](https://guidehouse.example.com/predator-solutions/2026-press) |

Guidehouse published its annual Predator Solutions market report, sizing the global market at $7.8B (up from $7.1B in 2025). **So what:**

- The "at risk of being outpaced by AI-native entrants" positioning validates the urgency of AnvilOS v3.0's AI targeting features — discuss whether the current roadmap timeline is aggressive enough
- Urban pest control growing at 28% confirms the market expansion opportunity but also increases urgency — Coyote Tech and others are building traction in this segment now

### City of Los Angeles Announces $50M Smart Pest Control Initiative

| | |
|---|---|
| **Topic** | Urban Pest Control Market |
| **Date** | 2026-02-12 |
| **Source** | [LA City Council Press Release](https://lacity.example.gov/smartpest) |

The City of Los Angeles announced a $50M initiative to deploy "smart pest control" technology across the city over the next three years. The RFP will be published in Q2 2026, with requirements including: quiet operation, zero collateral damage, real-time monitoring dashboards, and integration with city IoT infrastructure. The initiative is the largest municipal pest control technology contract announced to date.

**So what:**

- This is a concrete market signal validating the urban pest control opportunity — the RFP requirements (quiet, zero collateral damage, IoT integration) describe capabilities that drones and software platforms can meet but traditional anvils and explosives cannot
- Acme's current product portfolio is not competitive for this RFP without a lightweight urban product offering — discuss whether to pursue via partnership, acquisition, or internal development
