# Predator Solutions & Catching Technology — Industry Landscape

## Executive Summary

The predator solutions industry is in the midst of a technology-driven transformation. Historically dominated by hardware manufacturers (anvils, catapults, traps), the market is shifting rapidly toward AI-powered autonomous solutions and software platforms. This mirrors broader industrial automation trends, but with unique challenges around reliability in harsh environments, regulatory scrutiny of explosive and rocket-powered products, and the emerging "prey evasion" counter-market.

Three forces are reshaping the industry: (1) autonomous drone technology threatening traditional hardware, with VC investment exceeding $500M in the last three years; (2) increasing OSHA and wildlife regulatory scrutiny of traditional catching methods; and (3) the expansion into urban pest control, a $2B adjacent market with fundamentally different product requirements than the traditional desert predator segment.

For Acme Anvils, these trends directly affect all three strategic pillars: the AnvilOS digital transformation must keep pace with AI-native competitors, the Reliability Revolution must satisfy tightening regulatory standards, and market expansion into urban pest control requires product capabilities that traditional hardware cannot deliver.

## Current State

### Dominant Trends

- **Hardware-to-SaaS transition accelerating.** Industry-wide recurring revenue grew from 28% to 41% of total revenue between FY2023 and FY2025. Acme's 53% recurring mix puts it ahead of the industry average
- **VC investment surging in autonomous solutions.** Over $500M invested in AI/drone catching startups in the last three years, led by Coyote Tech's $125M in total funding
- **Regulatory tightening on traditional methods.** OSHA proposed new safety standards for explosive catching devices in Q4 2025, with a comment period closing March 2026

### Emerging Themes

- **Urban pest control as the next battleground.** Multiple startups and incumbents are eyeing the $2B urban market. Drones have a natural advantage here — traditional hardware faces fundamental constraints in dense environments
- **Prey evasion technology creating demand-side disruption.** Roadrunner Systems' "guaranteed catch zones" product suggests that the prey side of the market is innovating faster than the predator side — this could shrink the addressable market for all catching solutions

### Active Debates

- **Autonomous vs. human-assisted:** Is fully autonomous catching viable, or will the industry settle on human-in-the-loop with AI assistance? Reliability data suggests the latter, but VC funding flows toward the former
- **Regulation as opportunity vs. threat:** Will tighter OSHA standards hurt incumbents (compliance cost) or help them (barrier to entry for underfunded startups)?
- **Platform vs. point solution:** Should catching companies build ecosystems (like AnvilOS) or focus on best-of-breed single products?
