# Why I built this

Most AI tools for product managers help you write better PRDs or spin up prototypes. But few help you figure out whether you're building the right thing in the first place.

This project tries to close that gap.

## The context problem

LLMs give generic advice without comprehensive context. Ask an AI to evaluate a product opportunity and you'll get a bland, textbook answer unless you feed it your competitive landscape, your customers' pain points, your company's strategic priorities, and your market dynamics.

Product managers carry this context in their heads, scattered across documents, decks, and conversations. No tooling existed to help PMs capture and organise that context so AI could actually give useful, specific advice.

## Why these problems matter to me

My work sits at the intersection of strategy, product marketing, and product management. I haven't been directly involved with software development teams in recent roles — my focus is on the earlier part of the product development process: understanding markets, identifying opportunities, and deciding where to spend R&D budget. I wanted tools that would help accelerate that work with AI.

### Ramping up in new industries

I've spent most of my career in B2B SaaS, working across fintech, construction, accounting, and billing products — in a country with a relatively small SaaS industry. Specialising in a single sector isn't always possible when your market is small, so with each new role I've needed to learn a new industry and a new set of players from scratch.

PMs are expected to provide thought leadership about customer needs and opportunities. That's hard to do when you're still getting up to speed on the landscape. AI can compress that ramp-up significantly.

### Pressure-testing your thinking

It's always useful to have a trusted peer critique your assumptions. In practice, product managers are often too busy to regularly review each other's work. Product leaders struggle to find time to go deep with their teams, especially junior PMs. AI with the right context can fill that gap — not replacing human judgement, but providing a readily available sparring partner that applies product management best practices.

## What this project does

This repo gives PMs a structured way to capture their working context — competitive intelligence, industry landscape, personas, strategy — and a set of AI skills that use that context to do real analytical work.
