# Vision Document Template

Use this template for a deeper exploration of a specific strategic bet. Each vision document covers one bet — create a separate file for each. These live in `{company_dir}/product/vision/`.

## Frontmatter

```yaml
---
title: "Vision: {Name of Strategic Bet}"
confluence_title:
confluence_url:
---
```

## Structure

```markdown
# Vision: {Name of Strategic Bet}

## The Opportunity

{What's the market opportunity or strategic imperative? Include relevant data — TAM, competitive gaps, customer signals. 2–3 paragraphs.}

## The Hypothesis

**We believe that** {action or capability we're building}
**will allow us to** {outcome for customers or the business}
**which will result in** {measurable strategic impact}.

## What Success Looks Like

| Timeframe | Metric / Milestone | Target |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| {Near-term} | {Specific measure} | {Target value} |
| {Medium-term} | {Specific measure} | {Target value} |
| {Long-term} | {Specific measure} | {Target value} |

## Strategic Fit

{How does this bet connect to company strategy? Which strategic pillars does it support? What risks does it address?}

## Key Unknowns

| Unknown | Why it matters | How we'll learn |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| {Critical question} | {Impact if wrong} | {Planned approach to answer it} |

## Competitive Context

{How are competitors approaching this space? What's our advantage? Where are we behind?}

## Risks

{Bullet list of risks — things that could make this bet fail even if the hypothesis is correct. Include market risk, execution risk, and strategic risk.}
```

## Guidance

- Vision documents explore the "why and where" of a strategic bet. The roadmap covers the "what and when."
- The hypothesis section forces clarity: if you can't fill it in crisply, the bet isn't well-defined yet.
- Key Unknowns is the most actionable section — it feeds directly into the assumption-identifier and validation-suggester skills.
- Keep it to 1–2 pages. This is a strategic exploration, not a PRD.
