# Roadmap Template

Use this template for a strategic roadmap. The roadmap should be one integrated document covering the PM's full scope — not split per-product. The point is to show how competing priorities are balanced across shared capacity.

## Frontmatter

```yaml
---
title: Strategic Roadmap
period: {e.g., FY26}
team: {Owning team name}
confluence_title:
confluence_url:
---
```

## Structure

```markdown
# {Product/Platform} — Strategic Roadmap

## Context

{Brief description of what this roadmap covers, who owns it, and key resource constraints. This grounds readers in the PM's actual scope and capacity.}

## Now ({current period})

{Active work with committed resources and delivery targets. Use tables for initiatives:}

### {Initiative Name}

| Initiative | Target | Status |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| {Specific deliverable} | {Target date} | {Current status} |

**Dependencies:** {Cross-team or external dependencies}

## Next ({next period})

{Planned work with directional commitment. Scope and timing subject to current period results. Bullet points are fine here — less precision is appropriate for less certain work.}

## Later ({future period}+)

{Strategic direction. Not yet committed — depends on how Now and Next play out. Group by theme.}

## Trade-offs and Constraints

| Tension | How we're managing it |
|---------|----------------------|
| {Competing priority A vs. B} | {How you're allocating capacity and what triggers a re-evaluation} |

## What's NOT on this roadmap

{Explicitly call out related work that is owned elsewhere. This prevents confusion about scope boundaries.}
```

## Guidance

- **Now** should be specific: named initiatives, target dates, status, dependencies.
- **Next** should be directional: planned work contingent on current results.
- **Later** should be thematic: strategic direction without commitment.
- **Trade-offs** are the most valuable section — they reveal the real prioritisation logic.
- Include "What's NOT on this roadmap" to prevent scope confusion.
